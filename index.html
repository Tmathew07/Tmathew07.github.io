Fun Facts About The Planets

Sun

  1. Mercury

    • Closet planet from the sun.
    • Smallest planet in our solar system.
    • Named after the Roman god of messanger.
    • A day is 176 Earth days.
    • A year is 88 Earth days.

  2. Venus

    • Does not have any moons or rings.
    • Nearly as big as the Earth.
    • A day is 117 Earth days.
    • A year is 225 Earth days.
    • Hottest planet in our solar system.

  3. Earth

    • It has a 23.5 degree tilt.
    • Has four seasons: spring, summer, autumn (fall) and winter.
    • The densest planet in the solar system.
    • Has a strong magnetic field that protects it from solar winds.
    • Not named after a god.

  4. Mars

    • Named after Roman god of war.
    • Sunsets on Mars are blue.
    • Has the larest dust storms in the solar system.
    • Has two moons: Phobos and Deimos.
    • Has the tallest mountain in the solar system called Olympus Mons.

  5. Jupiter

    • The largest planet in the solar system.
    • The largest gas gaint in the solar system.
    • It's Great Red Spot is the largest eternal storm in the solar system.
    • It is the fastest rotating planet planet in the solar system.
    • It does not have a rocky surface.

  6. Saturn

    • Has the most number of moons in the solar system.
    • It has a hexagon shaped storm.
    • Its ring is made of large or small ice, dust, and rock.
    • Saturn has the lowest density of any planet in our solar system.
    • It is named after the roman god of farming.

  7. Uranus

    • A ice giant, the coldest planet in the solar system.
    • The planet rotates on its side.
    • Named after the Greek god of the sky.
    • The planet is blue because it contains methane.
    • It was the first planet to be discovered.

  8. Neptune

    • The eight plant from the sun.
    • The smallest gas gaint in the solar system.
    • A year is 165 Earth years.
    • Named after the Roman god of the sea.
    • Has 6 faint rings.
